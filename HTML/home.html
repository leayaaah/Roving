EVERYWHERE

Whose Trace?

At dawn, we stood where the sea met the cliffs—where mountain and ocean touched like old friends. The beach was quiet, untouched. But then we saw them: footprints in the sand, leading from the water up toward the mountain trail. Not fresh, but not yet erased by the waves. We looked around. No boats. No people. Just gulls crying and the wind whispering in the pine trees above. We followed the trail. Up the rocky path, past blooming wildflowers and stones stacked into strange towers. Shells were scattered on the mountainside—shells, here? As if the sea had climbed the slopes. At the summit, we found it: a single feather, long and silver, standing upright in a crack between two stones. Not bird, not anything we knew. Didn't answer. Just stood there, sea behind us, sky all around. Nature had spoken in riddles—waves in the sand, wind in the trees, signs only the wild could leave. And even now, when I return to that beach, I still wonder: ?
